Voting Resources

Everything you need to register, research, and show up — in one place.

Voter Registration & General Voting
Vote.gov
vote.gov
The U.S. government's official voting portal. Register by mail, online, or in person based on your state. Available in multiple languages — the most authoritative starting point for any voter.
Vote.org
vote.org
The largest nonprofit, nonpartisan voter registration and GOTV platform in America. Register in under 2 minutes, check your status, request an absentee ballot, find your polling place, and verify ID requirements.
USAGov Voting Hub
usa.gov/voting-and-elections
The federal government's comprehensive guide — registration deadlines, absentee and early voting, voter ID rules, sample ballots, and how to find your local election office. All 50 states covered.
Ballot & Candidate Research
Ballotpedia
ballotpedia.org
A deep-dive encyclopedia of American elections. Look up every candidate, ballot measure, election policy, redistricting maps, and historical results by state. Completely free and nonpartisan.
VOTE411
vote411.org
By the League of Women Voters. Enter your address to see your specific ballot, candidate survey responses, polling place info, and registration deadlines. Available in English and Spanish. Served 9.5 million users in 2024.
TurboVote
turbovote.org
Sends you text and email reminders for every election — from school board to president. Shows upcoming races, registration status, voter ID rules, and a sample ballot tailored to where you live.
Civic Organizations & Community Outreach
League of Women Voters
lwv.org
Founded in 1920, one of America's oldest nonpartisan civic organizations. Voter registration drives, advocacy, litigation to protect voting rights, and public education. 800+ state and local chapters.
When We All Vote
whenweallvote.org
Co-chaired by Michelle Obama. Focuses on closing the race and age voting gap. Offers a Voter Resources Hub to register, check status, and find polling places. Runs grassroots drives in all 50 states.
Rock the Vote
rockthevote.org
Uses pop culture, music, and digital tools to engage and register young voters ages 18–24. Has registered millions of first-time voters since 1990.
NAACP Voter Resources
naacp.org/voting
Nonpartisan voter guides and rights advocacy focused on ensuring Black communities and communities of color have full and equal access to the ballot. Covers federal, state, and local races.
Official Government Resources
U.S. Election Assistance Commission
eac.gov
Supports election administration. Provides state-by-state links to election offices, voter registration forms, and information on how to cast a ballot. Up-to-date rules for every state.
Federal Voting Assistance Program
fvap.gov
Specifically serves U.S. citizens living overseas, military members, and their families. Helps with overseas voter registration and absentee ballot requests from anywhere in the world.
Nevada Secretary of State
silverstateelections.nv.gov
The official Nevada portal for voter registration, polling places, and mail ballot requests. The direct source for all Nevada-specific voting rules and deadlines.
